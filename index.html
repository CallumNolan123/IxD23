A short history of Diaclone

Diaclone (ダイアクロン, Daiakuron) is a toyline by Takara Toys launched in 1980.
It consisted of transforming vehicles and robots piloted by miniature, magnet-shoed figures spun off from the prior Microman toy line.

How it became transformers

In 1983, the Hasbro toy company imported the micro-change line of Diaclone figures to become the first year of Transformers with story and character profiles written by Marvel writer, Bob Budiansky

In 1982, the line later featured the Car-Robots set of transforming robot toys, invented by Ōno Kōjin. While the original series featured robots and vehicles, Car-Robots added the feature of the robots being able to disguise themselves as various contemporary vehicles.
